# Fit-Med Clinical Medication Assessment

**Patient Details**

*   Age: 65
*   Gender: Male
*   Diagnosis: Acute Myeloid Leukemia, s/p Stem Cell Transplant (Day +8)
*   Smoking/Alcohol: Information not reported
*   Date of Assessment: 2025-09-17

**Medication Assessments**

*   **Medication:** Meropenem
    *   Indication: Febrile neutropenia, Complicated intra-abdominal infections, Complicated skin infections, Meningitis
    *   Benefits specific to patient: Broad-spectrum carbapenem coverage for potential infections in a neutropenic post-transplant patient. Excellent Gram-negative and Pseudomonas coverage.
    *   Risks & Interactions: Seizures (especially with renal impairment), Allergic reactions, *C. difficile*. Interacts with valproate, decreasing valproate levels and potentially causing seizures.
    *   Strength of evidence: Information not found in tool output.
    *   Risk Minimisation Measures (RMM): Renal dose adjustment, De-escalation when cultures available.
    *   Fit-Med Outcome: Favorable
    *   Recommendation: Continue meropenem, ensuring appropriate renal dose adjustment. Monitor for seizures and *C. difficile* infection. Be aware of potential interaction with valproate. De-escalate when cultures available.

*   **Medication:** Zavicefta
    *   Indication: MDR Gram-negative infections
    *   Benefits specific to patient: Covers KPC/OXA producers, strong Gram-negative activity.
    *   Risks & Interactions: Nephrotoxicity, *C. difficile*. Interacts with aminoglycosides, increasing the risk of nephrotoxicity.
    *   Strength of evidence: Information not found in tool output.
    *   Risk Minimisation Measures (RMM): Use only when MDR suspected/confirmed, Renal dose adjustment.
    *   Fit-Med Outcome: Unfavorable
    *   Recommendation: Discontinue Zavicefta unless multi-drug resistant Gram-negative infection is suspected or confirmed. If continued, ensure appropriate renal dose adjustment and avoid concomitant aminoglycosides. Monitor for *C. difficile* infection.

*   **Medication:** Fluconazole
    *   Indication: Candida infections, Antifungal prophylaxis post-transplant
    *   Benefits specific to patient: Strong Candida activity, convenient once-daily dosing for antifungal prophylaxis in a post-transplant patient.
    *   Risks & Interactions: Hepatotoxicity, QT prolongation, raises levels of many drugs. Interacts with cyclosporine, increasing cyclosporine levels, and amiodarone, increasing QT prolongation.
    *   Strength of evidence: Information not found in tool output.
    *   Risk Minimisation Measures (RMM): Monitor LFTs, ECG monitoring if QT risk.
    *   Fit-Med Outcome: Conditional
    *   Recommendation: Continue fluconazole for antifungal prophylaxis. Monitor LFTs and renal function. Monitor ECG if other QT-prolonging medications are used. Monitor cyclosporine levels closely due to potential interaction.

*   **Medication:** Valacyclovir
    *   Indication: HSV prophylaxis, VZV prophylaxis
    *   Benefits specific to patient: Prevents viral reactivation in a post-transplant patient. Good bioavailability.
    *   Risks & Interactions: Renal toxicity, neurotoxicity. Increased risk with concomitant nephrotoxic drugs.
    *   Strength of evidence: Information not found in tool output.
    *   Risk Minimisation Measures (RMM): Adjust dose in renal impairment.
    *   Fit-Med Outcome: Favorable
    *   Recommendation: Continue valacyclovir. Adjust dose based on renal function. Avoid concomitant nephrotoxic drugs if possible.

*   **Medication:** Cyclosporine
    *   Indication: GVHD prophylaxis, Transplant immunosuppression
    *   Benefits specific to patient: Prevents GVHD in a post-transplant patient.
    *   Risks & Interactions: Nephrotoxicity, hypertension, drug interactions. Interacts with azoles and macrolides, increasing cyclosporine levels, and amiodarone, increasing toxicity.
    *   Strength of evidence: Information not found in tool output.
    *   Risk Minimisation Measures (RMM): Therapeutic drug monitoring required.
    *   Fit-Med Outcome: Conditional
    *   Recommendation: Continue cyclosporine. Strict therapeutic drug monitoring is required. Monitor renal function and blood pressure. Be aware of potential interactions with azoles, macrolides, and amiodarone.

**Monitoring Protocol**

| Medication      | Monitoring Parameters                                  | Frequency        |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | ---------------- |
| Meropenem       | Renal function, Neurological status, *C. difficile* infection | Daily, As needed |
| Zavicefta       | Renal function, WBC, *C. difficile* infection         | Daily, As needed |
| Fluconazole     | LFTs, Renal function, ECG (if QT risk)                | Weekly, As needed|
| Valacyclovir    | Renal function                                        | Daily            |
| Cyclosporine    | Trough levels, Renal function, Blood pressure         | Daily, Weekly    |
